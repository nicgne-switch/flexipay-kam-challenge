Three strategic insights

01
You're not paying for Yuno. You're paying for a routing brain.

Every 1 point of blended approval = $124K/year of FlexiPay net revenue. Yuno's multi-acquirer routing has delivered 2–3 points of lift vs single-acquirer baseline over 18 months — that's $248K–$372K/year of value. dLocal's single-acquirer architecture in Peru alone puts this at risk.

02
Brazil is not a cost question. It's a speed question.

Yuno already supports PIX, Boleto, and BR local acquiring — 0 weeks of new integration. dLocal requires full re-integration: 6–8 weeks of your 4-dev team. Every week of delay is $2.5M of deferred TPV in the Base case and permanent share loss to competitors Sofia is racing against.

03
Your engineering constraint is the real story.

FlexiPay has 4 backend devs, 12 open roles, and Brazil on the line. dLocal migration consumes ~1,280 engineering hours of opportunity cost. Those same hours, applied to Yuno's Brazil fast-track, generate $990K of FlexiPay Year-1 upside. This is the highest-ROI decision FlexiPay will make this quarter.

Point-by-point response to dLocal

❖ "0.67% on cards = $370K savings"
Correction: Actual savings at Jan volumes = $427,920. CFO's number is understated. Headline evaporates before Month 3 once you price in the $1.13M of switching costs below.
❖ "Free Brazil integration — 20 hrs engineering"
20 hours is dLocal's effort, not FlexiPay's savings. FlexiPay still burns 120–160 hours on re-integration. Yuno counter: zero new integration — BR goes live in 3 weeks vs 6–8 weeks on dLocal.
❖ "PIX at 0.50% take rate"
dLocal 0.50% excludes FX markup, chargeback protection, dispute handling (sold separately). Yuno match: 0.50% PIX on 24-mo BR commit. Concession cost: $36K/yr. Trade paid for by expansion revenue.
❖ "24-month term, $25M/mo minimum"
FlexiPay Jan TPV = $20.7M. Shortfall = $4.3M/mo × 0.67% × 12 = $345K/year of commitment overage exposure. Yuno counter: 18-month term, no hard minimum, pricing review only if TPV drops >25% for 3 consecutive months.
❖ "Named AM, bi-weekly check-ins, Slack"
Feature Yuno already offers. FlexiPay has requested this 5 times — a service failure we need to fix, not a dLocal advantage. Dedicated Slack + weekly syncs + named CSM stood up by end of week.
❖ "$0 platform fee"
$24K/yr = 1.2% of FlexiPay's annual spend. Yuno waives platform fee entirely. Cost to Yuno: $24K. Value: disarms CFO's line-item objection.
